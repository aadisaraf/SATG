# Cold Email Tracking - SATG Research Mentorship

## Target Selection Criteria
- Active research in UDA, domain adaptation, semantic segmentation, synthetic-to-real transfer
- Assistant/Associate professors (higher response probability than senior faculty)
- Lab websites that mention mentorship/outreach
- Recent publications (2024-2026) directly connected to SATG methodology
- Balance of prestige institutions and more accessible ones

## Best Practices Summary
- **Length**: Under 150-200 words. Professors receive 50-200 emails/day.
- **Target**: Assistant/associate professors > senior faculty. Senior faculty have full labs and less bandwidth.
- **Personalization**: Reference a specific paper + specific finding/insight. Don't just say "I liked your work."
- **Subject line**: Professional, specific. e.g., "High School Student: Research Interest in [specific topic]"
- **AI markers to avoid**: "I am writing to express", "Furthermore/Moreover", "delve/explore", "passionate", "I've always been fascinated", "stood out", "struck me", perfect sentence structure, lack of contractions, synthetic enthusiasm
- **Do include**: Specific paper reference, clear connection to your work, brief and direct ask
- **Don't include**: Life story, grades, generic praise, long paragraphs
- **Timing**: Avoid finals (Dec, May), August (vacation). Best: early fall (Oct-Nov) or spring. Right after a new paper drops.
- **Follow up**: One follow-up after 7-10 days is fine. Two max.
- **Background**: Only include personal background if directly relevant to their work and research shows it increases reply likelihood.
