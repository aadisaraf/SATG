PART 1 — DEEP REASONING: ARE THE CURRENT STRUCTURAL CUES SUFFICIENT?
================================================================================

SHORT ANSWER: No. The current two-cue prior (edge density + local variance)
captures important but incomplete structural information. Two additional cues —
local entropy and structure tensor cornerness — are strongly motivated by
both theory and the specific failure modes of GTA5→Cityscapes UDA.
Adding them NOW, before precomputation, costs nothing extra in compute time
since the heatmaps haven't been generated yet.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT THE CURRENT PRIOR CAPTURES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EDGE DENSITY (Canny):
  Measures WHERE edges are — the COUNT of edge pixels in a local window.
  Strength: identifies regions with many object boundaries.
  Weakness: Canny is binary. A crisp, clear road-edge and a fuzzy,
  ambiguous occlusion boundary both register as "edge present."
  Also: misses the DIRECTION of edges entirely.

LOCAL VARIANCE:
  Measures HOW MUCH intensity varies in a local patch.
  Strength: captures textural complexity, rough surfaces, cluttered appearance.
  Weakness: variance is a second-moment statistic. It captures the SPREAD of
  intensity values but not their DISTRIBUTION. Two regions — one with 10 evenly
  distributed intensity values and one with values concentrated at two extremes —
  can have identical variance but very different structural complexity.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY THESE TWO CUES ARE INSUFFICIENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FAILURE CASE 1 — OCCLUSION BOUNDARIES:
  A pedestrian half-behind a pole. The boundary region has gradients from
  TWO directions simultaneously (the pedestrian edge + the pole edge).
  Edge density sees "there are edges here" — correct. Local variance sees
  "there is intensity variation" — correct. But neither captures the FACT
  that gradients are coming from multiple directions at once, which is the
  precise geometric signature of an occlusion. This is exactly the location
  where the teacher will assign the wrong class, and where the teacher's
  confidence is most dangerously miscalibrated.

FAILURE CASE 2 — DOMAIN-SHIFT VEGETATION:
  GTA5 trees are geometrically simple (repetitive polygon meshes).
  Cityscapes trees have complex, irregular leaf structure with many subtle
  intensity gradations. Edge density is moderately high in both. Local
  variance captures some difference. But what truly separates them is the
  DISTRIBUTIONAL RICHNESS of intensity values — Cityscapes vegetation has
  many distinct micro-level intensity values (high entropy) whereas GTA5
  vegetation has fewer distinct values arranged more predictably (lower
  entropy). Entropy is the right tool here.

FAILURE CASE 3 — COMPLEX BACKGROUNDS WITH CLEAN FOREGROUND:
  A sign post (structurally simple foreground) in front of a busy
  intersection (high-complexity background). The local window centered on
  the post edge captures BOTH the clean post boundary AND the chaotic
  background. Edge density and variance average out the complexity.
  But multi-directional gradient complexity (cornerness) specifically
  identifies the junction zones where boundaries from multiple objects
  overlap, even at pixel-level precision.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE TWO ADDITIONS AND THEIR PRECISE RATIONALE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ADDITION 1 — LOCAL SHANNON ENTROPY:

  Definition: H_ent[p] = -Σ_b p_b * log(p_b)
  where p_b is the fraction of pixels in a local window with intensity
  in bin b (using, e.g., 32 quantization bins).

  What it captures that variance CANNOT:
    - Variance = second statistical moment = spread from mean.
    - Entropy = information content = number of distinct states.
    - A 50/50 black-and-white striped patch has HIGH variance but
      LOW entropy (only two states). A gradient-rich, multi-textured
      patch has HIGH variance AND HIGH entropy.
    - For Cityscapes: the transition between vegetation and sky, or
      between building textures and road surface, produces the exact
      multi-state intensity distribution that entropy captures and
      variance underestimates.

  Where it matters most in GTA5→Cityscapes:
    - Vegetation (complex real-world texture vs. uniform synthetic)
    - Mixed-material backgrounds (building+vegetation+sign together)
    - Horizon zones where multiple semantic classes blend

  Implementation: skimage.filters.rank.entropy(gray_uint8, disk(radius))
    This is O(H*W*B) where B = number of bins, fully vectorized in skimage.
    Approximately 0.3–0.8 seconds per full-resolution Cityscapes image.

ADDITION 2 — STRUCTURE TENSOR MINIMUM EIGENVALUE (λ₂, CORNERNESS):

  Definition: The structure tensor at each pixel is J = blur(∇I ⊗ ∇I),
  where ∇I = (Gx, Gy) is the image gradient. J is a 2×2 matrix with
  components Jxx=blur(Gx²), Jxy=blur(Gx·Gy), Jyy=blur(Gy²).
  The minimum eigenvalue λ₂ = 0.5*(Jxx+Jyy) - 0.5*sqrt((Jxx-Jyy)² + 4Jxy²)

  What it captures that edge density CANNOT:
    - λ₂ ≈ 0: either a flat region (no gradient) or a clean edge
      (gradient all in one direction). Both are structurally simple.
    - λ₂ >> 0: gradients exist in MULTIPLE DIRECTIONS in the local window.
      This is the mathematical signature of: a corner, a junction between
      two boundaries, an occlusion boundary, or chaotic multi-object texture.
    - Edge density counts how many edges exist. λ₂ specifically identifies
      where edges from DIFFERENT directions intersect — the most dangerous
      locations for pseudo-label errors.

  Why this is uniquely valuable for UDA:
    - Under domain shift, the teacher's segmentation boundaries are most
      likely wrong at JUNCTIONS — where multiple objects meet. These are
      precisely where λ₂ is highest.
    - A clean road edge (λ₂ ≈ 0): teacher probably correct here.
    - A pedestrian in front of a building (λ₂ >> 0): teacher is navigating
      a multi-directional gradient field and is most likely to error.
    - Harris (1988) used λ₂ as a corner detector for exactly this reason:
      it is the classical measure of multi-directional gradient complexity.

  Implementation: cv2.Sobel for gradients → elementwise products →
  cv2.GaussianBlur → λ₂ formula. All vectorized. Very fast (~0.1s per image).

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CRITICAL DESIGN CHANGE: SEPARATE COMPONENT FILES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

With 4 cues, storing a single pre-combined heatmap is inefficient for ablation:
to run "edge-only", we'd need to re-run precomputation with edge_weight=1.0.
That's 2 hours of CPU time per ablation type.

BETTER DESIGN: Store 4 separate component .npy files per image.
Combine them at DATALOADER time using the weights in the current config.

  Per-image files saved to disk:
    {stem}_satg_edge.npy       — edge density [H,W] float32
    {stem}_satg_var.npy        — local variance [H,W] float32
    {stem}_satg_ent.npy        — local entropy [H,W] float32
    {stem}_satg_corn.npy       — cornerness λ₂ [H,W] float32

  At training time, CityscapesDataset does:
    H = w1*edge + w2*var + w3*ent + w4*corn     (all from config)
    H = clip(H, 0, 1).astype(float32)

  Effect on ablations:
    Ablation "edge-only": edge_weight=1.0, all others 0.0 → no re-precompute
    Ablation "variance-only": variance_weight=1.0, all others 0.0 → same
    Ablation "entropy-only": entropy_weight=1.0, all others 0.0 → same
    Ablation "cornerness-only": cornerness_weight=1.0, all others 0.0 → same
    Ablation "all-four equal": all 0.25 → same (default)

  Disk impact: 4× more files, but each is a float32 [H,W] ≈ 2MB per image.
  Total: 2975 images × 4 × 2MB ≈ 24GB. Ensure your data disk has space.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
UPDATED COMBINATION FORMULA AND CONFIG
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  H = w1*edge_density + w2*local_variance + w3*local_entropy + w4*cornerness
  with w1 + w2 + w3 + w4 = 1 (enforced by design; default: all 0.25)

  The constitution's constraint is satisfied: all four cues are derived from
  raw RGB pixels using classical deterministic CV operations with no learned
  parameters and no target-domain labels.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT WE ARE NOT ADDING AND WHY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

GRADIENT MAGNITUDE (Sobel continuous):
  Not added. Information is already largely captured by Canny edge density +
  cornerness (cornerness USES Sobel gradients internally). Adding it would
  create correlated features and muddle the ablation story.

GLCM TEXTURE FEATURES:
  Not added. Computationally expensive (O(H*W*K²*levels)) and their
  information content is largely subsumed by local entropy. Would add
  significant complexity for marginal gain.

MULTI-SCALE EDGE DENSITY:
  Not added. The 4-cue combination already provides multi-faceted structural
  information. Adding scale as a dimension increases the parameter count and
  makes ablations much more expensive.

CHROMINANCE VARIANCE / COLOR ENTROPY:
  Not added in this iteration. Worth noting as future work in the paper.
  Grayscale-based cues are sufficient for the claim; color adds complexity.

MONOCULAR DEPTH DISCONTINUITIES:
  Not added. Requires a learned model. Constitution violation (Section 1.5).

ADD two new private methods to StructuralPrior:

Method 1: _local_entropy(self, gray: np.ndarray) -> np.ndarray
  """
  Compute local Shannon entropy over a disk-shaped neighborhood.
  Uses skimage.filters.rank.entropy for efficient histogram-based computation.

  Args:
    gray: float32 [H, W] grayscale image, values in [0.0, 1.0]
  Returns:
    entropy_map: float32 [H, W], normalized to [0.0, 1.0]
  """
  Implementation:
    from skimage.filters.rank import entropy as rank_entropy
    from skimage.morphology import disk
    # rank_entropy requires uint8 input
    gray_uint8 = (gray * 255).clip(0, 255).astype(np.uint8)
    radius = self.cfg.structural_prior.entropy_kernel_radius   # default 7
    ent_map = rank_entropy(gray_uint8, disk(radius)).astype(np.float64)
    # rank_entropy output is in bits [0, log2(n_possible_values)]
    # normalize to [0, 1]
    ent_map = (ent_map - ent_map.min()) / (ent_map.max() - ent_map.min() + 1e-8)
    return ent_map.astype(np.float32)

Method 2: _structure_tensor_cornerness(self, gray: np.ndarray) -> np.ndarray
  """
  Compute minimum eigenvalue (λ₂) of the structure tensor at each pixel.
  High λ₂ indicates gradients from multiple directions simultaneously —
  the signature of occlusion boundaries, object junctions, and complex
  multi-object regions where pseudo-labels are most unreliable.

  Args:
    gray: float32 [H, W] grayscale image, values in [0.0, 1.0]
  Returns:
    cornerness: float32 [H, W], normalized to [0.0, 1.0]
  """
  Implementation:
    gray_f64 = gray.astype(np.float64)
    # Gradient computation
    Gx = cv2.Sobel(gray_f64, cv2.CV_64F, 1, 0, ksize=3)
    Gy = cv2.Sobel(gray_f64, cv2.CV_64F, 0, 1, ksize=3)
    # Structure tensor components (smoothed outer products)
    sigma = self.cfg.structural_prior.cornerness_sigma    # default 2.0
    ksize = self.cfg.structural_prior.cornerness_kernel_size  # default 15
    Jxx = cv2.GaussianBlur(Gx * Gx, (ksize, ksize), sigma)
    Jxy = cv2.GaussianBlur(Gx * Gy, (ksize, ksize), sigma)
    Jyy = cv2.GaussianBlur(Gy * Gy, (ksize, ksize), sigma)
    # Minimum eigenvalue: λ₂ = 0.5*(Jxx+Jyy) - 0.5*sqrt((Jxx-Jyy)²+4*Jxy²)
    trace = Jxx + Jyy
    discriminant = np.sqrt((Jxx - Jyy) ** 2 + 4.0 * Jxy ** 2 + 1e-12)
    lambda2 = 0.5 * (trace - discriminant)
    lambda2 = np.clip(lambda2, 0.0, None)   # λ₂ ≥ 0 by definition
    # Normalize
    lambda2_norm = (lambda2 - lambda2.min()) / (lambda2.max() - lambda2.min() + 1e-8)
    return lambda2_norm.astype(np.float32)

  for iteration in range(cfg.training.iterations):

    student.train()
    src_imgs, src_labels = next_source()
    src_imgs = src_imgs.to(device); src_labels = src_labels.to(device)
    src_main, src_aux = student(src_imgs)
    h, w = src_labels.shape[-2:]
    src_main = F.interpolate(src_main, (h,w), mode='bilinear', align_corners=False)
    src_aux  = F.interpolate(src_aux,  (h,w), mode='bilinear', align_corners=False)
    source_loss = (source_criterion(src_main, src_labels) +
                   cfg.training.aux_loss_weight * source_criterion(src_aux, src_labels))

    tgt_imgs, tgt_heatmaps = next(target_iter)
    tgt_imgs = tgt_imgs.to(device)
    tgt_heatmaps = tgt_heatmaps.to(device)   # [B,H,W] combined heatmap

    with torch.no_grad():
        tgt_t_main, _ = ema.model(tgt_imgs)
        tgt_h, tgt_w = tgt_heatmaps.shape[-2:]
        tgt_teacher_logits = F.interpolate(tgt_t_main, (tgt_h,tgt_w),
                                           mode='bilinear', align_corners=False)
        tgt_probs    = F.softmax(tgt_teacher_logits, dim=1)
        confidence   = tgt_probs.max(dim=1).values
        pseudo_labels = tgt_probs.argmax(dim=1)

    tgt_s_main, _ = student(tgt_imgs)
    tgt_student_logits = F.interpolate(tgt_s_main, (tgt_h,tgt_w),
                                       mode='bilinear', align_corners=False)

    if cfg.trust_gate.type == 'hard':
        tw = hard_gate.compute_mask(confidence, tgt_heatmaps)
        target_loss = satg_loss(tgt_student_logits, pseudo_labels, tw)
        trust_coverage = tw.mean().item()

    elif cfg.trust_gate.type == 'soft_weight':
        tw = soft_weight_gate.compute_weights(confidence, tgt_heatmaps)
        target_loss = satg_loss(tgt_student_logits, pseudo_labels, tw)
        trust_coverage = tw.mean().item()

    elif cfg.trust_gate.type == 'soft_label':
        st = soft_label_mod.compute_soft_targets(tgt_teacher_logits, tgt_heatmaps)
        cm = soft_label_mod.compute_confidence_mask(tgt_teacher_logits,
                                                    cfg.trust_gate.tau_conf)
        target_loss = soft_label_kl_loss(tgt_student_logits, st, cm)
        trust_coverage = cm.float().mean().item()
        with torch.no_grad():
            T = soft_label_mod.compute_temperature(tgt_heatmaps)
            mean_temp = T[cm.bool()].mean().item() if cm.sum() > 0 else 0.0

    total_loss = source_loss + cfg.training.lambda_target * target_loss
    optimizer.zero_grad()
    total_loss.backward()
    optimizer.step()
    scheduler.step()
    ema.update(student)

    if iteration % cfg.logging.log_every == 0:
        lr = scheduler.get_last_lr()[-1]
        row = {'iteration': iteration,
               'total_loss': round(total_loss.item(), 6),
               'source_loss': round(source_loss.item(), 6),
               'target_loss': round(target_loss.item(), 6),
               'trust_coverage_ratio': round(trust_coverage, 4),
               'mean_temperature': round(mean_temp, 4),
               'lr': round(lr, 8), 'val_miou': ''}
        csv_writer.writerow(row); csv_file.flush()
        if cfg.logging.backend == 'wandb':
            wandb.log({k:v for k,v in row.items() if v != ''}, step=iteration)

    if iteration % cfg.training.eval_interval == 0 and iteration > 0:
        student.eval()
        val_miou, per_class = compute_miou(student, val_loader, device)
        student.train()
        print(f"[{iteration}] val mIoU: {val_miou:.2f}%")
        csv_writer.writerow({'iteration': iteration, 'total_loss': '',
                             'source_loss': '', 'target_loss': '',
                             'trust_coverage_ratio': '', 'mean_temperature': '',
                             'lr': '', 'val_miou': round(val_miou, 4)})
        csv_file.flush()
        if cfg.logging.backend == 'wandb':
            wandb.log({'val/miou': val_miou,
                       **{f'val/{k}': v for k,v in per_class.items()}},
                      step=iteration)
        ckpt = {'model_state': student.state_dict(),
                'ema_state': ema.state_dict(),
                'optimizer_state': optimizer.state_dict(),
                'scheduler_state': scheduler.state_dict(),
                'iteration': iteration, 'best_miou': best_miou,
                'config': OmegaConf.to_container(cfg, resolve=True)}
        torch.save(ckpt, save_dir / 'last.pth')

            print(f"  ★ New best: {best_miou:.2f}%")

  csv_file.close()
  if cfg.logging.backend == 'wandb': wandb.finish()

def compute_miou(model, dataloader, device, num_classes=19, ignore_index=255):
  CITYSCAPES_19 = ['road','sidewalk','building','wall','fence','pole',
                   'traffic light','traffic sign','vegetation','terrain','sky',
                   'person','rider','car','truck','bus','train','motorcycle','bicycle']
  model.eval()
  confusion = torch.zeros(num_classes, num_classes, dtype=torch.long)
  with torch.no_grad():
      for imgs, labels in dataloader:
          imgs, labels = imgs.to(device), labels.to(device)
          main_logits, _ = model(imgs)
          preds = F.interpolate(main_logits, labels.shape[-2:],
                                mode='bilinear', align_corners=False).argmax(1)
          mask = labels != ignore_index
          for t, p in zip(labels[mask].cpu(), preds[mask].cpu()):
              confusion[t, p] += 1
  iou = {}
  for c in range(num_classes):
      tp = confusion[c,c].item()
      fp = confusion[:,c].sum().item() - tp
      fn = confusion[c,:].sum().item() - tp
      d  = tp + fp + fn
      iou[CITYSCAPES_19[c]] = (tp/d*100) if d > 0 else 0.0
  return sum(iou.values())/num_classes, iou
